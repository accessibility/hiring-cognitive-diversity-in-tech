Cognitive diversity is an umbrella term for changes that occur within the brain that are considered to be normal variations or a normal result of challenging life experiences. These changes allow people to process information in different and often new and creative ways. The cognitively diverse will often process and address problems differently, see details others might not notice, ask question others might not think to ask and more. This means that cognitive diversity can be a largely a positive thing, especially in a supportive environment with diversity and inclusion in mind.

In this section you'll find testimonials from technologists who identify as cognitively diverse. These were gathered via a small qualitative study, initiated by Larunda Accessibility Solutions via the 2021 axe-hackathon event. Our hope is to see this research funded so that it can be expanded to include a more diverse range of cognitive diversities and provide even more remedies for hiring professionals. Where we shine!

The aptitudes of those with different cognitive-wiring styles can be ideal for tech and design. Let's take a look at the gifts that Neurodivergent people can bring to the table. Below you will find strengths for just a few populations included in the NEURODIVERGENT umbrella.

Just remember: People may have one or more types of neurodivergence, and as an individual, their talents will be unique to them. So please, as important as it is to know how valuable Neurodivergent people are for the workplace, try your best to avoid using any stereotype, positive or negative, when making assumptions about any specific applicant.

General Skills of Neurominorities

  • Neurominorities are less impacted by group think and approach problems from new angles, making us original thinkers and the lifeblood of innovation.
  • If our work is in alignment with out special interests and/or affinities, we will often outperform our neurotypical peers, sometimes by a large margin.

Skills of People with ADHD

  • Sparkling personality - bright, creative, sense of humor, they get along with others, inspire engaging conversations, make a workplace fun and increase team morale.
  • Ethics - generosity, compassion, and a strong sense of fairness
  • Willingness to take risks
  • Spontaneity
  • Persistence
  • Highly creative
  • Contagious motivation and energy

Skills of Autistic People

  • Deep-dive expertise through attention to detail, deep focus, advanced observation skills, ability to absorb and retain large amounts of information, and a methodical self-guided approach to exploration and learning
  • Visual skills
  • Creativity - novel approaches to problem solving
  • Tenacity and resilience
  • Ethics - Accepting of differences and high integrity

Skills of Dyslexic People

  • Improved pattern recognition - finding the odd one out, seeing how things connect and form complex systems, and identifying similarities and differences
  • Advanced spatial reasoning
  • Sharper peripheral vision - take in whole scenes quickly, visual thinking, and big-picture recognition
  • Business entrepreneurs
  • Highly creative
  • Thinking-outside-the-box problem solvers

Skills of People with OCD

  • Phenomenal attention to detail - noticing patterns and changes, perfection driven
  • Goal oriented and committed to top quality completion
  • Compassionate
  • Excellent designers of systems and project planners
  • Explain things well and in detail

Skills of People with PTSD

  • Enhanced state of sensory sensitivity resulting in increased pattern recognition, attention to environmental details and risk assessment
  • May noticed effects and details that others are not consciously aware of and so can't articulate
  • Threat Hunting
  • Vulnerability analysis

As you can see, Neurodivergent people can provide strong skills to add to your teams. The value flows across the tech landscape: researchers, designers, developers, inventors, makers, graphic designers, computer programmers, software and web developers, managers, artists, accessibility specialists, and entrepreneurs of all types.
